The Gate · Day 90

What "Go" looks like

5 / 15
Buyer endorsement
At least five Western buyer interviewees score 4 or 5 on the endorsement rubric — "would push internally" or "would recognize publicly."
8 / 15
Supplier willingness
At least eight Chinese supplier interviewees say "I'd pay something meaningful" for a verified-supplier credential.
AND · plus
  • HK Limited incorporated, bank account open, founders' agreement signed
  • Signed data-sharing MOUs with Longda and Future Forge
  • Export-controls + China data counsel consults complete, written compliance posture in hand
  • One named Western buyer agrees in principle to receive a "reference shipment" in Phase 2
  • Written Go/No-Go memo signed by all three founders

If fewer than 5/15 buyers and 8/15 suppliers reach the threshold: pivot or stop. Don't push through on hope. That's a successful Phase 1, not a failed company.

Operating system

The weekly cadence

Every Monday · 30 min — Founders' standup. Each person: what I shipped last week, what I'm shipping this week, what I'm blocked on. Async over Slack if calendars don't align.
Every Friday · 1 hour — Interview debrief + rubric review. What we heard, what's working, what to change.
Every other Sunday · 1 hour solo — Patrick's strategic check-in. Macro picture, are we still building the right thing? Writes a short memo to share Monday.
Kill triggers

Red flags that mean stop sooner than Day 90

By Day 30 · if Patrick's employment counsel says he can't legally hold founder equity in TrueHeat while at his current job — restructure or pause until he leaves.
By Day 45 · if 10 buyer interviews have happened and zero are scoring 4 or 5 — the wedge is wrong. Pivot or stop.
By Day 60 · if Longda OR Future Forge has gone cold on the MOU, replace them with a Plan B supplier before spending Phase 2 budget.
At any time · if export-controls or China data counsel raises a structural compliance issue we can't engineer around — that may be the real go/no-go.

The whole point of stage gates is to spend cheap in Phase 1 so the expensive Phase 2 commits are well-grounded. If we get to Day 90 and the data says No, that's a successful Phase 1 — not a failed company.
